Data Analysis Results

Our geographic map gave us some information – but we want to know if there is more to the story. So we looked a little deeper at this relationship. Our first chart is just a scatterplot of the components of the affordability index. Again – you can see that California and Hawaii are outliers (good reasonableness check on the two charts). You can also see that there does seem to be a pattern of both median salaries and median house prices increasing together. You could draw a gently positively sloped regression line that would do a pretty good job of capturing this relationship. The problem is, the slope isn’t large enough to maintain a constant affordability index. Initial inspection suggests that while salary does increase as housing prices increase – it does NOT increase as quickly. For that to be the case, you would need to have a regression line through any of these points with a slope equal to the affordability index. Our next graph further illustrates this suspicion.
